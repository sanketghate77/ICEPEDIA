Anatomy

Main Components

Every internal combustion engine shares a core set of precision-engineered parts working in harmony.

COMPONENT / 01
Piston

The piston reciprocates inside the cylinder, converting the energy of expanding gases into mechanical motion. Made from aluminium alloy for a perfect balance of strength and weight.

COMPONENT / 02
Crankshaft

Converts the linear back-and-forth motion of the piston into the rotational motion used to drive the wheels. One of the most highly stressed components in the engine.

COMPONENT / 03
Spark Plug

Generates a precisely-timed electrical spark to ignite the compressed air-fuel mixture. Fires thousands of times per minute under extreme heat and pressure conditions.

COMPONENT / 04
Valves

Intake valves allow the air-fuel mixture in; exhaust valves let burnt gases escape. Controlled by the camshaft, they open and close with extraordinary precision at high RPM.

COMPONENT / 05
Connecting Rod

Links the piston to the crankshaft, transmitting the force of combustion. Must endure immense compressive, tensile, and bending loads across millions of cycles.

COMPONENT / 06
Engine Block & Head

The block houses the cylinders, coolant passages, and oil galleries. The cylinder head contains the valves and combustion chambers, bolted to the block with a head gasket seal.

Where It's Used

Applications

The internal combustion engine powers a vast range of machines across transport, industry, and beyond.

🚗
Automobiles

Cars, SUVs, and light trucks — the most common application of the ICE worldwide.

✈️
Aircraft

Piston engines power light aircraft and early aviation, still used in general aviation today.

🚢
Marine

Large diesel ICEs power ships, ferries, submarines, and offshore vessels globally.

🏍️
Motorcycles

Single and twin-cylinder engines deliver high power-to-weight ratios for two-wheelers.

🏗️
Heavy Machinery

Excavators, bulldozers, cranes, and industrial generators all run on powerful ICEs.
